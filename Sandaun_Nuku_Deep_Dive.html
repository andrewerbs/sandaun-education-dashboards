Why Nuku is the first call on resources

Nuku is the structural pinch-point of Sandaun. It carries the worst staffing (68 understaffed schools, median PTR 37.6), the worst classrooms (37% bush material — bush rooms outnumber permanent ones), the worst feeder loss at the Grade 2→Grade 3 seam (64%, against a province average of 70%), and the highest overall risk (74 schools at three or more flags, average risk 2.43). When the Board allocates teachers, houses and classroom rebuilds, Nuku should be first in line.

How many children, and in which grades?

Nuku starts with the second-largest Prep cohort in the province (6,097) but loses children faster than anywhere else. By Grade 3 enrolment has already fallen to 2,473 — the early-grade bulge collapses early and hard.

Where do children leave between grades?

Each bar is the share of one grade’s children who appear in the next grade. The Grade 2→Grade 3 step is highlighted in red. In Nuku that step is 64% — the worst in the province, with a feeder ratio of roughly 24% between elementary and primary schools. This single seam is where the district haemorrhages children.

G2→G3 = 64%

The worst transition in Sandaun — 1,377 children lost between Grade 2 (3,850) and Grade 3 (2,473) in a single step. The feeder ratio between elementary and primary schools is about 24%.

The G7→G8 bar above 100% is a synthetic-cohort artefact — re-entry, repetition or in-migration recorded in a single census year — not real growth.

Do girls keep pace as grades rise?

The gender parity index sits below the province line from the start and slides to 0.72 by Grade 8. In Nuku the gender gap rides on top of the heaviest dropout, so girls are lost at the worst point in the system.

How bad are staffing and buildings?

Nuku is at or near the bottom on every structural measure. The snapshot below reads against province benchmarks — the district trails on classrooms, female staffing and risk, and carries the most schools with no staff house at all.

Priority actions for Nuku in 2026

Nuku is where province-level investment should land first — staff the schools, build the houses, rebuild the classrooms.

Operations — staff the worst first

Redeploy teachers to the 68 understaffed schools, starting with single-teacher schools carrying the heaviest loads. Target the Grade 2→Grade 3 feeder seam where loss is worst in the province.

Resources — houses & classrooms

Build staff housing at the 59 schools with none and rebuild bush-material classrooms (37% of rooms). Housing and safe rooms are the precondition for any staffing gain holding.

Performance — track the seam

Report the G2→G3 transition and the risk-flag count each term. Recruit and house female teachers (only 35%) and track GPI to Grade 8 in the highest-risk schools.
