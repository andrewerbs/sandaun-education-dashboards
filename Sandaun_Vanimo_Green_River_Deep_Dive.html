Why Vanimo/Green River must be read with care

Vanimo/Green River is the provincial-capital district and the largest by enrolment — 31,023 students across 152 schools. It carries the best gender parity in the province (Grade 8 GPI 0.87) but also the largest absolute housing shortfall (331 houses). Its headline numbers come with a caution: Vanimo town high schools draw students in from across the province, so the upper grades are inflated by in-migration and the transition figures should not be read as real cohort growth.

How many children, and in which grades?

Enrolment falls through the primary years like everywhere else, but then jumps back up at Grade 7 and Grade 8 — Vanimo town high schools pull students in from other districts. The upper-grade bars are not a sign that more local children are staying; they reflect in-migration to the capital.

Where do children leave between grades?

Each bar is the share of one grade’s children who appear in the next grade. The Grade 2→Grade 3 step is highlighted in red (69%). The Grade 6→Grade 7 bar reaches 181% — this is not real growth but in-migration into Vanimo town schools, the clearest example of a synthetic-cohort artefact in the province.

Do girls keep pace as grades rise?

Vanimo/Green River holds gender parity better than any other district. The index dips through mid-primary but recovers to 0.87 by Grade 8 — the best Grade 8 parity in the province and well above the province line at the top end. The urban-coastal setting keeps girls in school later.

What does the staffing-and-housing picture show?

As the largest district, Vanimo/Green River carries the biggest absolute gaps — most notably 331 houses short, the largest shortfall in the province. Female staffing is close to the province average and classrooms are in better shape than the inland districts. The snapshot below reads against province benchmarks.

Priority actions for Vanimo/Green River in 2026

Tackle the housing backlog at scale, hold the gender-parity advantage, and report upper-grade figures honestly.

Operations — plan for the draw

Plan teacher and classroom capacity in Vanimo town high schools for the in-migrating Grade 7–8 cohort, while still targeting the local G2→G3 seam (69%) where children leave.

Resources — clear the housing backlog

Prioritise the 331-house gap, starting with the 52 schools that have none. As the largest district, this is where the province’s biggest absolute housing investment is needed.

Performance — report honestly

Hold the best-in-province Grade 8 parity (0.87) and report upper-grade transition with the in-migration caveat, so the 181% G6→G7 figure is never read as local growth.
