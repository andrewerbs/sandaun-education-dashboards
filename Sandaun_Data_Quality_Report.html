Can we trust the 2025 census?

Mostly yes — but with named exceptions the Board must verify before acting on them. 524 schools returned a census; coverage is good. The problems are concentrated: a handful of impossible records, predictable missing fields on power and water, and a teacher count that comes out three different ways depending on which raw table you sum. This report lists every exception by name so the figures used elsewhere in the suite can be read with confidence.

How many schools, and how many rows did we start with?

The census arrives as three separate raw tables, and none of them holds exactly one row per school. Some schools appear twice under a repeated EMIS code; teacher details run one row per teacher. We de-duplicated EMIS codes (keeping the row with the larger enrolment) so analysis runs on one row per school.

De-duplication rule: where one EMIS code appeared more than once, we kept the row with the larger enrolment. This drops 524 returned schools to 521 with usable enrolment.

Which records are impossible on their face?

Some rows describe situations that cannot exist — students with no teacher, or one teacher against hundreds of pupils. These are not findings about the schools; they are flags for verification. Each is named below so the Board can field-check rather than guess.

One enrolment outlier inflating the pupil–teacher ratio

Holy Trinity / Baro Primary — PTR 358

Records 4,656 enrolment against 13 teachers. A PTR of 358 is almost certainly a data-entry error with enrolment overstated. Treat this school’s enrolment as unverified until checked.

Which fields are most often missing?

Missing data clusters in predictable places — the infrastructure and teacher-registration fields that enumerators reach last. We treat the sentinel value 999 as missing, and “Information missing” or blank cells as failing. Electricity is the worst offender at 106 schools.

How many teachers does the province have? It depends how you count.

Three methods each produce a defensible total, and they disagree by over 300. We do not pick one silently — the Board should know which number is being quoted and why. Summing per-school Prep–G8 staff gives the most conservative figure; the compiled headline is the highest.

The 333-teacher spread between the lowest (2,011) and highest (2,344) reflects teachers counted on the summary return but not listed individually, and grade-staff totals that include non-classroom roles. Until reconciled, quote 2,131 for per-school analysis and state the source.

Why do some grades appear to grow more than 100%?

A few grade-to-grade transition ratios exceed 100% — the province as a whole shows G7→G8 at 107%, and Vanimo/Green River shows G6→G7 at 181%. These are not real growth. They are artefacts of comparing different grades in a single census year, where re-entry, repetition and in-migration inflate the upper grade.

What we are doingComparing this year’s Grade 7 with this year’s Grade 8 — two different sets of children, not the same cohort tracked over time.
Why it can exceed 100%Repeaters, returning drop-outs, and children moving in from other areas can make an upper grade larger than the lower grade was this year.

Treat survival and transition figures across the suite as synthetic-cohort estimates from one census, not as tracked cohorts. They show the shape of attrition, not exact head-counts of individual children.

What to fix before the 2026 return

Three moves would close most of these gaps at source, so the next census needs far less cleaning.

Validation rules at data entry

Reject impossible rows on entry: enrolment with zero teachers, a PTR above ~80, or a single teacher above 60 pupils. Block 999 and blanks on key fields, and flag duplicate EMIS codes before submission.

Field-verify the named schools

Visit Mapul Primary (ghost school), the 10 single-teacher schools above 100 pupils, and Holy Trinity / Baro Primary (PTR 358). Confirm enrolment and staffing on the ground, then correct the record.

Make key fields mandatory

Require electricity, water source, and teacher registration before a return can be accepted — the fields missing at 106, 141 and 53 schools respectively. No submission should pass with these blank.
