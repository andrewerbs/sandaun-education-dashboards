Can the Annual School Census measure the PEIP?

The Provincial Education Implementation Plan 2023–2027 sets 113 indicators across nine focus areas. This report rates each one against a single question: can we report it honestly from the 2025 Annual School Census, or do we need another data system? The verdict from the planning workshop is blunt — the census alone can only ever cover about a quarter of the framework. The rest live in TFF reports, the curriculum division, assessment programmes, audit, governance records, and dedicated registries. Knowing which is which is the first step to a PEIP we can actually monitor.

Green — 16 indicators (14%)Fully reportable from census fields today: teacher qualifications, pupil–teacher ratios, toilets, libraries, class size, registration. Mostly in FA4 Teachers and FA8 Management.
Amber — 24 indicators (21%)Partial: a proxy, a sub-component, or a caveat. The census gets close but cannot stand alone — usually because a denominator or a quality measure is missing.
Red — 73 indicators (65%)Not in the census. Enrolment ratios need population data; learning quality, finance, governance and special registers all sit in other systems entirely.
Practical capability — about 25%Counting each Amber as half a point, the census today supports roughly a quarter of the framework. The single biggest unlock is school-age population by ward.

Which focus areas does the census cover?

Coverage is very uneven across the nine focus areas plus the baseline section. Teachers (FA4) and Management & Administration (FA8) are the strongest — the census was built around schools, staff and facilities. Early Childhood Education (FA1) and Citizenship & Values (FA9) are the weakest, with no green indicators at all. Access (FA2) carries the most amber because so many of its indicators are enrolment ratios waiting on a population denominator.

Why so much is red: the census was never built to carry the whole plan

The 73 red indicators are not failures of data entry — they belong to other systems by design. The census counts schools, students, staff and facilities on one day a year. It cannot see population, learning outcomes, money, governance, or the children who never enrol. Each red cluster below names the data owner who can report it instead.

Enrolment ratios (GER / NER)Every gross and net enrolment-ratio indicator needs a school-age population denominator by ward. The census has the numerator (children enrolled) but not the population it must be divided by. Owner: provincial population data.
Curriculum & assessment (FA5)Learning quality — literacy, numeracy, curriculum rollout — comes from CDD, the Outcomes & Learning Assessment, and PILNA. The census records facilities, not what children can do. Owner: CDD / OLA / PILNA.
Finance & auditTFF flows, school grants and acquittals sit in TFF reports and audit findings, not in the census return. Owner: TFF reporting & audit.
Governance (FA7 / FA8)Board functionality, inspections and management standards come from inspection and board records. Owner: inspection & governance records.
Special registersSpecial-needs education, the out-of-school children registry, and FODE / vocational enrolment each have dedicated registries the census does not reach. Owner: special-ed, out-of-school & FODE registers.
Secondary & vocationalSub-components beyond Prep–Grade 8 fall outside the basic-education census frame entirely. Owner: secondary & vocational reporting (TSC and others).

The unlock: how to monitor more of the PEIP

The census will never carry the whole plan on its own — nor should it. But three moves would lift monitoring from a quarter of the framework toward something the Board can govern with. The first is the largest single unlock available.

Population by ward = the biggest unlock

Provincial school-age population by ward would convert every GER and NER (enrolment-ratio) indicator from Red to reportable in one step — it is the missing denominator behind the largest red cluster in the framework.

1 — Add school-age population by ward

Secure provincial school-age population by ward from the population data system. This single dataset turns the enrolment-ratio indicators (the heart of FA2 Access and FA3 Equity) from Red to reportable, the largest gain available for one input.

2 — Negotiate cross-system data

Agree routine data-sharing with the owners of the other systems: TFF reports for finance, CDD / OLA / PILNA for learning quality, the out-of-school and special-ed registries, and TSC for the teacher workforce. Most red indicators already exist somewhere — they just need to reach the Board.

3 — Improve the census form

Add a small number of fields to the census return to lift amber indicators to green: the proxies and sub-components that are nearly there. Targeted form changes are cheap and tighten the quarter we already report.
