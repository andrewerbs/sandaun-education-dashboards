What the census says about the teaching workforce

Sandaun has 2,131 teachers spread across 521 schools that returned enrolment. The quality of the workforce is not the problem — 86% are fully qualified. The problems are distribution and conditions: too many children per teacher in Nuku and Vanimo/Green River, a registration backlog that has little to do with skill, too few women in the classroom, and nowhere for teachers to live.

Are there enough teachers?

For the province as a whole the median school runs 35 pupils per teacher (PTR) — close to the national target — but the mean is 42, dragged up by a long tail of overloaded schools. 192 of 521 schools (37%) exceed 40 PTR. Nuku and Vanimo/Green River carry the heaviest load; Telefomin, with the smallest schools, has the lowest pupil–teacher ratio.

Are teachers qualified?

86% of teachers (1,735) are fully qualified, with most holding a Primary Diploma or Elementary Certificate. Only 216 teachers (11%) are under- or partially-qualified. The gaps recorded elsewhere in this dashboard are in registration and housing rather than in qualifications.

Why are so many teachers unregistered?

69% of teachers hold full registration, but 27% (538) are only provisionally registered and a further 39 are still in training. This is not a quality problem — 96% are TSC-employed. The bottleneck is access to the Provincial Registration Committee (PRC) office: teachers in remote schools simply cannot get their paperwork processed.

39 schools

have 75% or more of their teachers on provisional registration — concentrated in the hardest-to-reach areas. The fix is a mobile PRC service, not retraining.

How many teachers are women — and does it matter?

Sixty per cent of teachers are male (1,215) and 40% female (788). It matters because the share of female teachers tracks the gender parity of pupils: Telefomin has the fewest women in the classroom (28%) and the lowest Grade 8 parity (GPI 0.646), while Vanimo/Green River has the most women (41%) and the highest parity (GPI 0.866).

Staff housing

Against ~2,251 teachers there are only about 1,300 houses — a shortfall of roughly 951. 200 of 500 schools (40%) have no staff house at all, and only 140 (28%) meet the one-house-per-teacher standard.
