Telefomin at a glance

Telefomin is the remote highlands district. Its staffing measures are the strongest in the province — the lowest median pupil–teacher ratio (31.8) and the lowest overall risk (average 2.05). Grade 8 gender parity is the lowest in Sandaun at 0.65, and 28% of teachers are female — the fewest in the province.

How many children, and in which grades?

Telefomin is the smallest district by enrolment. The early grades hold up reasonably, but the upper-primary drop is steep — enrolment more than halves between Grade 6 (1,034) and Grade 7 (454), the steepest upper-grade fall in the province.

Where do children leave between grades?

Each bar is the share of one grade’s children who appear in the next grade. The Grade 2→Grade 3 step is highlighted in red (69%), but the steepest drop is Grade 6→Grade 7, where only 44% carry forward — a near-total upper-grade fall alongside the gender gap.

G2→G3 = 69%

585 children lost between Grade 2 (1,888) and Grade 3 (1,303). But the steeper drop is later: only 44% of Grade 6 children reach Grade 7.

The G7→G8 bar above 100% is a synthetic-cohort artefact — re-entry, repetition or in-migration recorded in a single census year — not real growth.

Do girls keep pace as grades rise?

Parity is near balance in the early grades but falls to 0.65 by Grade 8 — the lowest Grade 8 gender parity in the province. Girls’ enrolment falls faster than boys’ at the upper-grade point where the cohort thins.

What does the staffing-and-housing picture show?

Telefomin’s ratios and overall risk are the strongest in the province, but its female-teacher share is the lowest and 44% of its schools have no staff house — the highest share in Sandaun. The snapshot below reads against province benchmarks.
