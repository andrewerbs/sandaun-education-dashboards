Why Aitape/Lumi is the benchmark

On nearly every measure the Provincial Education Board tracks, Aitape/Lumi sits at the top: the strongest Grade 2→Grade 3 transition (81% against a province average of 70%), the best staffing, the lowest share of bush-material classrooms, and the highest share of female teachers. It is the natural model for what the rest of the province can reach. The one warning sign is the same one that runs through the whole province — girls keep pace early but slip away by Grade 8, where parity falls to 0.73.

How many children, and in which grades?

Enrolment is largest in the early grades and thins through the primary years — the same shape as the province, but with a gentler slope. Even here, 527 children disappear between Grade 2 and Grade 3, the single sharpest step.

Where do children leave between grades?

Each bar is the share of one grade’s children who appear in the next grade. The Grade 2→Grade 3 step — the move from elementary to primary — is highlighted in red. In Aitape/Lumi that step is 81%, the best in the province, but it is still the district’s weakest internal boundary. The Grade 6→Grade 7 dip to 62% is the other watch point.

G2→G3 = 81%

The best transition in Sandaun — 527 children lost at this step, against more than 1,000 in the larger districts. This is the seam to protect, not fix.

Bars above 100% (Prep→G1, G5→G6, G7→G8) are synthetic-cohort artefacts — re-entry, repetition or in-migration recorded in a single census year — not real growth.

Do girls keep pace as grades rise?

The gender parity index (girls per boy enrolled) starts near balance and holds well through mid-primary, but falls to 0.73 by Grade 8 — below the province line at the top end. Even the benchmark district loses girls fastest in the final grades.

How do staffing and buildings compare?

Aitape/Lumi leads the province on classrooms and female staffing, but still carries a real housing gap and a block of higher-risk schools. The snapshot below reads against province benchmarks — green where the district is ahead, amber where it still has work.

Priority actions for Aitape/Lumi in 2026

Hold the lead, close the housing gap, and protect girls through the upper grades.

Operations — document the model

Capture what drives the 81% G2→G3 transition and the low bush-classroom share, and use Aitape/Lumi as the worked example for Nuku and Telefomin. Watch the Grade 6→Grade 7 dip (62%).

Resources — finish the houses

Target the 48 schools with zero staff houses to lock in the staffing advantage. Closing the 251-house gap keeps teachers in place and protects the district’s lead on PTR.

Performance — hold girls to G8

Track GPI by grade and report retention of girls to Grade 8 as the headline outcome. Use the 48% female workforce to mentor and retain girls through the upper-primary grades.
